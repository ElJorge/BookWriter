## 🟡 Genuine NEW findings (recommend fixing)

### N1 — Ch24: "TVA holds" — undefined stray acronym
*"Then Devang, who never warmed, warm: **\"TVA holds. Chain holds. You've got it.\"**"* — "TVA" appears **exactly once in the whole book** and decodes to nothing; the engine/system is otherwise "the engine / the machine / the system / the reader." It reads as a stray. **Suggested fix:** replace with an established in-world referent, e.g., *"The spoof holds. Chain holds. You've got it."* or *"Window holds. Chain holds. You've got it."* (Confirm what Devang means it to be.)

### N2 — Ch4: "forty-five impossible miles south" → should be "minutes"
The recurring incantation measures **Theo's drive time** (~45 min WeHo→Simi). Ch3 line 19: *"forty-five impossible **minutes** south."* Ch4 line 17: *"forty-five impossible **miles** south."* The Ch4 "miles" is the outlier and breaks the refrain. **Suggested fix:** Ch4 → "minutes."

### N3 — Ch18: "the room … into a room" — word-repetition stutter
*"the one that breathed chilled air **into a room** with no light"* sits a few words after "the room," reading as an accidental echo. **Suggested fix:** "…breathed chilled air into a **space** with no light" (or recast).

### N4 — Ch2↔Ch5: the two-truck wrinkle
Ch2 establishes **Cole drives his own truck** from Hermosa to the house ("he has the truck"; "gotten into the truck, and started driving"), where **Russ's truck** already sits in the driveway (the cooling-block clue). Ch5: *"The truck wasn't his to take and he took it"* — Cole leaves in **Russ's** truck, and his own is never addressed (two trucks at the scene; his is abandoned). The text knows it's Russ's truck, so it's not a flat contradiction, but the logic gap is noticeable. **Options:** (a) have Cole leave in his own truck; (b) add a half-line acknowledging he leaves his own (e.g., why Russ's — blocking him in, or the page is in it); (c) accept as-is. Author's call.

### N5 — Ch26: "Six hands / Seven people" — the count doesn't visibly close
*"Cole's… Theo's… Two she didn't have names for yet. And Russ's… **Six hands. Seven people**…"* — the enumerated list gives **5 hands** (Cole + Theo + two + Russ), not six; and "seven people" isn't traceable from the five named. **Suggested fix:** reconcile — e.g., make the unnamed "Two" explicitly contribute the missing hands ("Two more, four hands between them"), or adjust the totals so the prose and the enumeration agree.

## 🟢 Minor / optional (LOW)

- **L-a — "water" simile cluster.** *"the flood pouring over the unbearable thing the way water covers a stone"* is **verbatim** in Ch8 and Ch9 (parallel key-hand-off scenes); plus "water finding a crack" (Ch25) / "water finding the lowest floor" (Ch26). Intentional motif, but the verbatim Ch8/Ch9 repeat reads a touch copy-paste. Optional: vary one.
- **L-b — Ch19 "refused for days."** *"one cold-room field on his board had refused **for days** to become a name."* The Rhea/Cold-Security thread reads as compressed to roughly a day-plus (Ch16 "thirty hours"). "for days" may overstate. Verify against the intended Movement-Two timeline; if tight, "for hours" / "all night."
- **L-c — Ch2/Ch3 two-clocks warmth.** Ch2 calls Russ's truck "driven and then, very recently, stopped" (warm), while Ch3's two-clocks clue needs the truck to read as the **older** clock vs. the warm coffee. Reconcilable (a block ticks as it cools), but Ch2's "very recently" slightly undercuts the puzzle. Optional: soften to "a while ago, stopped."
- **L-d — Russ's smuggle echo.** *"fix that thing in the kitchen… low and sl—"* recurs near-verbatim Ch8 & Ch9 — deliberate tic, but keep an eye that it doesn't appear a third time and read as a loop.
