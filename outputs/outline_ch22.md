# Chapter 22 Outline

## Meta
- **POV:** Rhea Okonkwo-Dale — close third (her SECOND POV; first was Ch17). Movement Two co-lead.
- **Setting:** The crew's working room (Marco's archive corner / Devang's rig), deep in a short night. The clock — jumped in Ch21 — runs visible and shrinking. Rhea has come in from the pharmacy lone-credential world into a room full of strangers. Atmosphere: the hush of people building a thing carefully and fast, the dread of a window closing, the absurd warmth of a fortress being renovated by people who weren't invited.
- **Opening Image:** A record bearing ONE name. Concretely: the chained record drafted so far — the disclosure form, the entry, the credential block — with a single signature line, RHEA OKONKWO-DALE, the only legible name in the chain, clean and whole and alone (the way her name has always been on the after-hours log: one hand, one key, one name accountable). She reads her own name printed there, total and naked, and it looks exactly like every solitary signature of her life — and that is the terror, because this time it is also the target.
- **Chapter Hook:** The shared record is nearly complete and survivable — and then the setback lands: the system's acceptance comes back conditional. The record will be accepted ONLY if it is SUBMITTED IN PERSON at the live site, inside the dark window — a human hand carrying it into the cold-room facility. The "who carries it" answer arrives snatched mid-word, dying on the dash, propelling the final build (Ch23) and locating the climax.
- **Thematic Undercurrent:** The sovereign-of-one, who organized an entire life around needing no one, must do the hardest thing — let strangers stand in the open WITH her and accept being held. The chain does not REMOVE the cost of exposure; it SHARES it. Consent, not capture (Donna's "she gets a vote" pays off). Mutual nakedness makes the unbearable survivable.

## Assumptions
- **Why Rhea POV:** She IS the source the weapon is built from; the chapter's core act — giving the total disclosure that makes her unforgeable AND naked, then ACCEPTING being held by strangers — is an interior event that can only be felt from inside her. Her plain-complete way of seeing (she omits nothing; her name forms clean) provides the exact contrast the chapter needs: the woman who says everything watching Russ, who can't say anything. Co-lead status earned in Ch17 is consolidated here.
- **Eaten-word rendering:** Rhea has NO eraser. The cold-room target stays unnamed via the WORLD/engine eating it (Russ's article-buckle witnessed from her outside vantage; the form-field that refuses the noun), never via a tidy authorial dodge. Rhea's name stays CLEAN throughout.
- **Setback chosen (one):** SUBMISSION IN PERSON at the live site inside the dark window. This locates the Ch24–25 climax (Osbourne St./Sylmar cold-room facility), keeps the cost mounting, and forces the Ch23 final build (the carry). It is NOT resolved here.
- **Warm beat budget:** ONE total — spent on the shared-exposure heart (the crew's disclosure), routed through Devang's single warm landing. (Per spec: my call.)
- **Cole:** Word stays BANKED. Pressure ratcheted HARD — the crew turns to him for the "go" more than once; he withholds. Fires Ch24–25.
- **Open/close rhyme:** A record/signature bearing ONE name (open) → the same record bearing MANY names, the single signature line grown into a column (close) — rendered through Rhea's perception, never narrator-named.

## Beat Breakdown

### Beat 1 — One Name, Clean (open the rhyme; the threshold becomes the task)
- **What happens:** Rhea, alone with the drafted record under the short clock, reads her own name on the single signature line — RHEA OKONKWO-DALE — total, legible, the only name in the chain. Establish her fortress-of-one habits through how she SEES: she has signed alone her whole working life (the after-hours log, one credential, one key, the satisfaction of a thing that needs no second hand). The record is nearly finished; what remains is the act she reached the threshold of in Ch21 — to actually GIVE the total disclosure, omit nothing, and become the source. Both registers: the dread (her name as a clean target) and the comedy (the room around her, strangers fussing over her like she's a difficult houseplant, Marco re-collating, Theo over-narrating the kettle).
- **Emotional target:** Recognition and unease — the reader feels the loneliness she has always called freedom, and senses it is about to be priced.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV); ambient crew (Marco, Theo) at edges.
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Rhea's voice = plain, complete, exact. She names things fully where Russ can't. Mine her professional precision (dosages, schedules, the calm of someone who has counted alone). The signature line is the rhyme object — render it through her eyes, no narrator pointing at it.

### Beat 2 — The Cost Made Real (total disclosure = total exposure)
- **What happens:** Devang and Marco walk her through what "total" means: the record must omit NOTHING to be unforgeable (Devang's spec-exploit — the self-approving engine MUST accept a valid input, and only completeness makes it valid). Rhea understands, plainly and completely, that disclosing everything makes her UNFORGEABLE and NAKED in the same stroke — every fact of her, legible, fixed, targetable. This is the Ch18 cost coming due. Render the genuine terror: the sovereign-of-one becoming fully readable to the very engine that selected her FOR being legible. She has spent a life making sure no one could spend her; this hands the engine the complete spec. Comedy stays alive in the bleakness (Marco's CAPS itemizing her exposure like a packing list; the gallows joke of being "thorough to death").
- **Emotional target:** Cold fear — the price is real, not abstract; the reader wants her to refuse.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), Devang (systems framing), Marco (CAPS/numbered list).
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Devang systems-cool here, NOT the warm beat. Let Rhea's plainness make the terror worse — she states her own nakedness accurately, no flinch in the words, all the flinch underneath. Marco's list can itemize and then an item dies on "—" (the thing the form can't hold).

### Beat 3 — She Gets a Vote (Donna's mirror-reckoning pays off)
- **What happens:** Donna steps in — in CUES, not sentences — and does NOT push Rhea over the threshold. She reads Rhea TRUE (mirror-of-love: sees the fortress, sees the fear, sees the real woman under both) and puts the choice in Rhea's hands. Donna's Ch16 rule lands: "We knock. We don't pick the lock. She gets a vote." Rhea is offered consent, not capture. The explicit contrast: the collector only ever SPENDS people (Russ was a key); Donna asks. Rhea — stunned that being asked is even on the table — feels the weight of an actual choice, the thing no one has ever extended to her. She does not answer yet (hold the vote for Beat 5, after she sees what she's being asked to trust).
- **Emotional target:** Aching dignity — the relief and vertigo of being CHOSEN-with rather than used.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), Donna (cues), crew watching.
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Donna in cues only — a tilt, a placed object, three words max. The payoff of "she gets a vote" should be ENACTED, not quoted as a slogan. Let Rhea register that no one has ever knocked before.

### Beat 4 — Naked With Her (the crew shares the exposure — the heart; thesis made flesh)
- **What happens:** Before Rhea votes, the crew answers the cost by SHARING it. One by one, in their own idiom, each becomes a NODE in the chained record — each discloses, each stands in the open WITH her, so erasing her means erasing ALL of them, and the lone-target engine still can't model a many. Concrete acts, NOT a speech: Marco adds his name and a true thing to the chain — his numbered list COMPLETES again as a node lands (callback to Ch20's "I never kept anything ALONE"); Theo discloses (his FREEZE offered as credential, his Ch21 weld holding — he gives the worst true thing about himself to the record); Devang — the ONE warm beat — frames it as a system and then lands human: distributing the exposure across nodes isn't just architecture, it's that none of them will be alone in it (his single warm landing). Cole: the crew turns to him for the node / the "go" — he gives PRESENCE (a knuckle on the table, a breath, his name added wordless) and WITHHOLDS the word; pressure ratchets. Rhea — the woman who never let anyone in — WATCHES strangers choose to be naked with her. The thesis made flesh: the chain doesn't remove the cost; it SHARES it.
- **Emotional target:** Overwhelm and warmth breaking through dread — the reader feels the many become a body around the one.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), Marco, Theo, Devang, Cole, Donna.
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Each disclosure in-idiom and SHORT. Marco CAPS/numbered; Theo apologetic-but-steady; Devang the single warm beat (earn it, don't repeat the register elsewhere). Cole's "go"-withholding must read as pressure rising toward the climax, not as failure. Render from Rhea's outside vantage — she catalogs each stranger's choice with her exact eye.

### Beat 5 — Russ Can't Say It (his wound; the many carry the one)
- **What happens:** Russ tries to add his node — to disclose, to put a true thing in the chain like the others — and BUCKLES. His eraser eats the true nouns; he reaches the article and the word gives out ("these aren't units, they're —"); the cold-room target stays UNNAMED, eaten by him in front of everyone. Rhea — who says everything, who has just been asked to say ALL of it — watches a man who physically cannot. Her plain-complete vantage makes his silence visible as a wound, not a flaw. The crew COMPLETES around him: someone adds his node FOR him, or holds the silence as itself a disclosure (the many carrying the one who can't say it). This is the moment that decides Rhea — seeing that the chain holds even the member who can't disclose, she understands being held: it is not earning your place by performance; it is being carried. SHE VOTES. She gives the total disclosure — chooses exposure, chooses to be the source, chooses to be held. (The vote from Beat 3 lands HERE, earned.)
- **Emotional target:** Heartbreak turning to resolve — Russ's ache, then Rhea's yes; the reader exhales into the choice.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), Russ (eaten-word, witnessed from outside), crew completing around him.
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Russ's article-buckle from Rhea's OUTSIDE view — she clocks the exact place the word dies. KEEP the cold-room target unnamed (the buckle does the work). Rhea's vote is the act, not a declaration — render it as her hand on the signature line, her saying the complete true thing she's been holding. Her name stays CLEAN.

### Beat 6 — The Chain Closes Around Her (rhyme transforms; record nears completion)
- **What happens:** The record fills. The single signature line has become a COLUMN — many names, many true things, many hands, distributed and witnessed (Marco's archive logic: un-erasable, many-handed). Rhea sees her one name no longer alone — surrounded, shared, the exposure spread thin enough across the many to be survivable. The open-image rhyme transforms: one name, clean and alone → many names, clean and together. She feels, for the first time, HELD — and the strangeness of it (comedy: she keeps reflexively reaching to do the next step alone and there are five hands already there; the fortress fully, absurdly, renovated). Devang/Marco confirm: the chained record is nearly complete; the engine should have to accept it and cannot erase it. The weapon is ALMOST done. Beat of false summit — survivable, shared, nearly there.
- **Emotional target:** Earned warmth and rising hope — the many-as-one body, the weapon nearly built.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), full crew.
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Land the rhyme through Rhea's perception ONLY — she sees the column of names where there was one; no narrator naming the echo. Keep one comedic beat (her solitary reflexes overruled by the crowd) so the warmth doesn't go cozy. This is a FALSE summit — let the reader feel "almost" before Beat 7 breaks it.

### Beat 7 — Accepted, Conditionally (the setback; submission in person)
- **What happens:** They submit the record / test acceptance — and the engine's answer comes back NOT clean. Acceptance is CONDITIONAL: the chained record is valid and un-erasable, but the spec requires it be SUBMITTED IN PERSON at the live site, inside the dark window — a human hand must physically carry it into the cold-room facility (Osbourne St./Sylmar) during the maintenance window. The very completeness that makes it unforgeable also fixes WHERE it must enter. The clock and the place lock together: the weapon is nearly complete, but the final build (Ch23) is now the CARRY — and the carry means one of them walks into the room. The cost mounts: sharing the exposure made it survivable, but someone still has to stand at the door alone. (Setback chosen; do NOT resolve — set up Ch23 and locate the climax.)
- **Emotional target:** Dread snapping back through the warmth — the price wasn't paid in full; there's a door yet.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), Devang/Marco delivering the condition, full crew.
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Engine rendered from OUTSIDE (courteous, frictionless, no breath). The condition should feel like the system being exactly itself — responding, not negotiating. Keep both registers (a grim absurdity in being "approved, conditions apply").

### Beat 8 — Who Carries It (close the rhyme; the eaten-word hook on the dash)
- **What happens:** The question that decides everything: who carries the record into the dark window. The crew turns — and the natural answer is the one whose disclosure is the record's spine, or the courier whose whole job is carrying. Eyes go to Cole. Pressure ratchets to its highest — they need the "go," the answer, the word — and as the answer (a name, the carrier's identity, the location detail, or a fresh threat from the engine reacting to their submission) begins to land, it is SNATCHED mid-word, dying on the dash. Rhea's last sight: the record bearing many names now, no longer one — held in a hand about to carry it somewhere she can't yet follow. Forward-pull into Ch23 (the carry / final build).
- **Emotional target:** Vertigo and propulsion — the weapon nearly complete, the door named, the answer torn away.
- **Characters present:** Rhea (POV), full crew, Cole (turned-to, withholding the word).
- **Notes for character_crafter:** END on the eaten-word hook dying on the dash — do NOT name the device/dash. Cole's word stays BANKED (if eyes go to him for the "go," he must NOT speak — the hook can be the answer cut off by EVENT, not by Cole finally talking). Close the rhyme: many names in the hand that carries. Do NOT over-resolve.
