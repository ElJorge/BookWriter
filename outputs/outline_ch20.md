The counter is not strength — it is INDELIBILITY held in MANY HANDS: a distributed memory of the HOLES that a lone-target-hunting eraser never modeled and cannot fully delete. A many vs a system built to erase the unconnected.

## Beat Breakdown

### Beat 1 — THE BLANK FIELD (open; Marco intake of Devang's thread)
- **What happens:** Marco takes Devang's Ch19 trace-dump into the archive. Establish the man through method: he alphabetizes his dread, numbers his panic, cross-refs the unknown to make it hold still. He logs the dump as a new card and confronts a BLANK FIELD on it — and we learn his lifelong axiom: a blank field means "pending / not yet catalogued," never "gone." Comedy of a man who needs a noun for the thing trying to kill him so he can FILE it. Horror seeded under the order: he can feel the dump is too clean, like something already swept it.
- **Emotional target:** Wry recognition tipping into unease — the calm of a man organizing on the edge of a cliff.
- **Characters present:** Marco (POV); Devang (comms, dry handoff); Donna (one cue).
- **Notes for character_crafter:** Marco's idiom hot from line one — CAPS for the field labels, a numbered intake list, an em-dash that fractures when a noun won't come. The blank field is the rhyme object; render his benign reading of it so the close can invert it. Both registers.

The comedy of vindication ("I KEPT these — I KNEW the noise meant something") curdling as the count climbs.
- **Emotional target:** The thrill of the catalog paying off, going cold.
- **Characters present:** Marco (POV); Theo (comms, softened over-talk trying to keep it light); Cole (wordless — a knuckle-tap pacing him).
- **Notes for character_crafter:** This is the method-as-detective beat. Numbered matches; each line a small win that adds up to a horror. Cole's tap is a metronome under Marco's escalation — pressure ratcheting, word still banked.
