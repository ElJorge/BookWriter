She got her family back and her hands won't stop. Crew texture: Theo over-talking too soft, Devang reabsorbing the network deadpan, Marco archiving the warm rescue, Cole wordless and watchful, Russ branded and off to the side. - **Donna (POV)**
  - Subtext: Relief curdling into a second weight. The job is done and her body won't believe it; killing the phones is the only way her hands know to say "home." She keeps the live one lit because she already knows she's not allowed to put it down — and resents that she knows.
  - Unspoken want: to sit. to be done. to be a person whose family is back and whose hands are empty.
  - Stated want: to retire the rig clean — every phone dark, the headcount closed.
  - Voice cadence: interiority is CUE-shaped — counting, load-bearing. "Fourteen. Pull. Thirteen." She narrates the world in items that can be carried or set down. Land her budgeted crack ONCE here: a single line that the rescue cost more than it won (interior, not spoken — e.g. she clocks the hole in Theo's talk and thinks the win has a hole in it the same shape). Re-seal immediately by returning to the count.
  - Physical expression: thumbs popping battery covers; lining dead phones in a row like a body count laid out gentle; her hand passing OVER the live one each pass and not landing. She does not sit. She cannot sit.
- **Theo**
  - Subtext: the keystone took the thing in him that used to fill silence on reflex. He reaches for the joke out of muscle memory and the muscle is torn.
  - Unspoken want: to be the room's pressure valve again, to prove he's still that.
  - Stated want: to keep the room loose, to narrate the teardown.
  - Voice cadence: thinned over-talk — starts a riff, the riff has a HOLE where the punchline goes, he loses the thread mid-sentence and lets it drop. Do NOT give him a clean joke. He almost lands one about retiring the phones ("fourteen funerals, no casseroles —") and the thread just... goes.

- **Devang** — reabsorbing the network, deadpan; quiet systems housekeeping. Comic-dry, not warm yet (warm is banked for Beat 3). A line about decommissioning nodes that's funny because it's clinical over a thing that was just life-and-death.

- **Cole** — wordless, watchful; the only one whose stillness reads as attention not exhaustion. He has clocked the live phone. He says nothing, of course.
- **Russ** — branded, off to the side, quiet; present but in the periphery of Donna's attention, a tool not yet picked up.

**Relationship dynamic:** Donna as foreman to a crew coming down off the adrenaline — she manages them by not looking at them too hard, giving them the dignity of the teardown. The power dynamic is gentle command; she's the one still standing because foremen don't get to fall first.

### Beat 2 — The Name That Won't Drown
**Structural summary:** Donna thumbs the live phone awake. RHEA OKONKWO-DALE. Coordinates. A credential string. **Character annotations:**
- **Donna (POV)**
  - Subtext: she's testing the name the way you test a board before you put weight on it — and it holds, and that's wrong, because everything the collector touches has a give in it somewhere. A clean name with no give is a chain with no links. She builds chains; she knows what an unlinked person is.
  - Unspoken want: for the name to buckle — to behave like the cold-room nouns, to refuse itself, to give her the cover of horror she knows how to feel.
  - Stated want: to read the assignment. to know what's on the card.
  - Voice cadence: she says the name ALOUD as a test — flat, foreman-checking-a-weld. "Rhea Okonkwo-Dale." Then again, quieter, because it held. The horror is that it's easy to say. Interiority stays CUE-clipped: a clean name, no friction; an unlinked person; she knows what that means.
  - Physical expression: she says it and waits for the noun-death that doesn't come; her jaw set for a buckle that never arrives. She sets the phone down and it stays lit, accusing.
- **Marco**
  - Subtext: the archive is his faith and the name just slid through it leaving no mark; that's not relief, that's the floor missing.
  - Unspoken want: to find the snag — the alias, the entanglement, the link — because a person with no snag can't be protected by the only tool he has.
  - Stated want: to log her, fully, into the record.
  - Voice cadence: CAPS / em-dash fracture. `> marco:` if remote, prose-in-quotes CAPS if in room. e.g. `> marco: NO ALIASES — NO PRIORS — NO CO-SIGNERS — SHE DOESN'T... THE RECORD WON'T HOLD ONTO HER. NOTHING TO GRAB.`
  - Physical expression (if in room): hands flat on the table, the archivist's body that's used to weight pushing on nothing.
- **Cole** — present, wordless. He watches Donna say the name and watches it hold. A single breath or tap that registers he, too, clocked that it didn't buckle. The wordless one notices the un-eaten word loudest.

**THE EATEN-WORD CONTRAST (load-bearing this beat):** The cold-room people surface ONCE here via euphemism-buckle — the noun dies, "the room that was full and then wasn't" — and that buckle is the ruler against which Rhea's clean name is measured. Cold-room people = UNNAMED, eaten. Rhea = NAMED, printed clean. The page itself enacts the inversion: one noun drowns, one name floats.

**Relationship dynamic:** Donna and Marco both reaching for friction and both coming up empty — foreman and archivist, two people whose entire trade is finding the give in a thing, defeated by smoothness. Quiet shared dread.
**Dialogue direction:** Minimal. Donna's test-aloud is the centerpiece. Marco's fracture lands the "nothing to grab." The comedy is thin here by design — maybe a single dark Devang/Theo aside ("most people you'd kill to find this little; her, it's the problem") — dread carries the beat.

**GALLOWS BEAT (vent the pressure):** Someone — Theo if present, else Devang dark-dry — notes you can't build a chain out of a person who keeps her own emergency contact set to herself. Dark, true, funny. It vents the ache without dissolving it. (Rhea's dignity preserved: the joke is on the IMPOSSIBILITY of the task, never on her as pitiable.)

**Relationship dynamic:** The crew as a many staring at a one who is, by design, a one — the thesis inverted in their faces. Devang's warmth pierces the room's clinical work; Donna absorbs it as the foreman who now knows the assignment has no template.

### Beat 4 — The Mirror Looks Into the Mirror
**Structural summary:** Donna's reckoning — the moral/tactical core. She reads flawed hands TRUE and BUILDS chains. The collector picked the ONE target her gift can't simply wrap: wrapping a chain around someone who has refused/lacked one is, from outside, indistinguishable from what the collector does — a hand reaching uninvited, deciding for her. The new strategy is aimed at HER gift specifically. She sets a rule as a CUE (we knock; we don't pick the lock; she gets a vote) and privately doesn't believe it survives the clock. To save Rhea she may have to become, for one move, the thing she fights.

**Character annotations:**
- **Donna (POV) — deepest interior of the chapter**
  - Subtext: the vertigo of the double. Her whole life she's told herself the difference between her and the men who reach into lives uninvited is CONSENT — she reads a hand and offers, she doesn't take. The collector just built a target where offering and taking look identical from the outside, because Rhea, alone by choice, has no one to ratify the offer. There is no witness to make Donna's reach a gift instead of a theft. The mirror: her gift IS the attack surface. The collector didn't find a weakness in the crew; he found the weakness inside Donna's virtue.
  - Unspoken want: to be told there's a clean way to do this — a version where she helps Rhea without overriding her. She wants absolution she can't give herself.
  - Stated want: to set the approach rule and move.
  - Voice cadence: the rule comes out as a CUE, three beats, command not confession: "We knock. We don't pick the lock. She gets a vote." Flat, foreman, final-sounding. The doubt NEVER reaches her mouth — it lives only in interiority and body. KEEP HER LACONIC. The depth is in what she won't say. Do NOT let her articulate the philosophical problem cleanly out loud; she feels it as a wrongness in the shape of the job.
  - Physical expression: she gives the cue and her hand is already moving to the next task before anyone can question it — armor. Maybe she looks at the live phone (the name that won't drown) the way you look at a door you swore you'd never open and are about to. A single tell: she sets her rule and then can't quite let go of the phone — proof the rule is armor over an open question.
  - **MIRROR-RECKONING — KEEP UNRESOLVED:** Do NOT resolve. The cue is a refusal masked as resolve. Annotate the tension; she proceeds SICK about it. The clock (collector's) is the unspoken reason the rule won't hold: Rhea can't be given the time to choose freely, so "she gets a vote" is a vote on a timeline Donna controls — which is exactly the theft she's swearing off. Donna senses this and does not say it. Leave the blade pointed at her virtue, in the air, into Ch17.
- **Crew in orbit** — present but not central; they take the cue as a cue (orders), not hearing the question underneath it. The dramatic irony: they hear command; the reader and Donna hear doubt. ONE of them (Theo, thinned) might almost ask "and if she says no?" — and not finish it, the thinned over-talk losing the thread right where the unbearable question lives.

**Relationship dynamic:** Donna vs. her own reflection — the collector as off-page mirror. With the crew, it's command-over-doubt: she leads by sealing the crack so they can keep working. Power dynamic = she protects them from the question by carrying it alone.
**Dialogue direction:** Spare to the bone. The CUE-rule is the only "thesis" line and it must read as armor, not answer. Interiority does the heavy lifting; keep it CUE-shaped (wrongness in the job's shape, not an essay). The comedy here is nearly absent by design — but allow Theo's unfinished "and if she says no —" to carry a sliver of gallows by being the joke that can't be made because it's too true. Dread register: MORAL, not action.
