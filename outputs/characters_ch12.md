## RULING #1 — Depth of the interior eaten-word (Russ's re-cracked POV)

**Rule: MARROW depth — the device lives in his interiority, not just his speech, and it works because his coping mechanism IS the eraser.**

How it works from INSIDE his head without him noticing:
- Russ narrates catastrophe like a work order to hold it still. That habit — "make it smaller" — is mechanically the same gesture as the eaten word. When the unbearable true noun rises, his interior voice does NOT stutter, does NOT trail off into "—". It **substitutes a smaller, domestic, service-call word and finishes that sentence cleanly.** He completes the bit. The reader completes the word.
- Example shape (for scene-weaver, do not lift verbatim): seeing the rows in the cold, he thinks *"Inventory. Long-term storage. Climate's holding at — call it spec, hold it at spec, that's all it is, units in a row, somebody's units —"* and the true word (people / the held) never arrives because "units" arrived in its place and the sentence is structurally satisfied. The horror is that it scans as competent.
- **He does not perceive the gap.** No "—I couldn't think of the word." That would be him noticing. The whole point: the man cannot afford the word, so the word is gone before it forms, and his own mind hands him a work-order synonym so smoothly he feels CALM, not blocked. The cost is invisible to him and visible to us.
- Frequency / escalation: seed faintly in Beat 1 (low stakes — "nothing to f—" handled as "nothing to service, nothing on the other side to fix," he moves on). Press in Beat 4 (the substitution does the heaviest lifting — "units," "manifest," "storage" stand in for the true thing). In Beats 5–6 the device is also turned OUTWARD and weaponized on the burner (he eats a word deliberately to withhold from the listening collector — and THAT one he DOES notice, because it's a choice). Final hook (Beat 6) is PERSON-eaten: the job snatches him mid-reveal; the half-formed target word cuts.
- **Distinction to hold:** interior eaten word = involuntary, unnoticed, his coping voice swallowing it (the tell of coming apart while staying flat). Burner eaten word = voluntary, noticed, a tradecraft choice. Both present; do not blur them. The contrast is itself characterization — the only word he chooses to eat is one he eats to protect them; the words his mind eats on its own are the ones that would protect HIM from knowing.

## RULING #2 — Cole's silence vs. burner speech

**Rule: Cole stays WORDLESS on the burner. Object-only and action-only. One (1) near-word that dies in his throat — unsent, unspoken — and that is the entire budget.**

- Cole does NOT speak a line on the burner. Not even a "copy." His presence registers as physical fact relayed by others ("Cole's on the fence," "Cole's got it") and as the wordless hand-off.
- The near-word: at the breach, the only thing close to speech Cole produces is the held canon line trying to surface — *you don't spiral, you just witness* — and it does not become audible. Render it as a breath, a shape of the mouth Theo half-sees, a thing he almost says to Theo and instead **hands** him the tool/keycard. The object IS the sentence.
- Theo/Cole motif re-tested at the breach: the wordless object hand-off (Ch11 echo) is the thing that actually gets the team THROUGH the perimeter. It WORKS. It is NOT reconciliation. Theo over-talks; Cole acts; the scar holds. Do not let the hand-off thaw into a moment — it is function, not forgiveness. Theo may even thank him and get nothing back but the next physical task.

## Annotated Beats

### Beat 1 — Palm on the Door (open rhyme; POV justification in the body)
**Structural summary:** Russ inside the man-trap, gloved hand flat on the inner cold-room door, reading it for heat out of pure habit. The door gives nothing — cold all the way through, nothing to fix on the other side. The badge log says he belongs. The window is RUNNING (a soft elapsed tone in the ear). The collector is a quiet presence. Russ talks himself through it like a service call. "Low and slow." The calm is a tool he's using ON HIMSELF.

**Character annotations:**
- **Russ**
  - Subtext: He is terrified and the terror has been re-filed as a job. The hand-on-door is genuine habit AND a man clutching the only ritual that holds him still.
  - Unspoken want: For the door to be a unit — a problem with a fix — so he can be a technician and not a criminal. Stated want (to himself): complete the read, do it right, low and slow.
  - Voice cadence: Warm service-call flatness, present tense of the hands. Short declaratives. First INTERIOR eaten-word seeds here — "nothing to f—" resolves cleanly into "nothing to service, nothing on the other side to fix," and he moves on without noticing the swap.
  - Physical expression: Flat palm, fingers spread, the trained pause. Breath paced to the elapsed tone. Stillness as armor.
- **The collector** (earpiece, minimal)
  - Subtext: None to mine — that's the point. He is at zero cost.
  - Unspoken want: The ticket moving. Stated want: nothing yet; presence only.
  - Voice cadence: Quiet, courteous, frictionless. A single unhurried acknowledgment, no menace in volume.
  - Physical expression: None (voice only). His CALM is the body — rendered as the absence of effort.

**Relationship dynamic:** Russ vs. his own competence; the collector as ambient pressure, not yet an interlocutor. The door is the third party — the thing Russ reads instead of reading his situation.
**Dialogue direction:** Almost no spoken line. Interior monologue carries. If the collector speaks, ONE clause, courteous, marking presence. Re-crack EARNED through hands and habit only — no exposition.

### Beat 2 — The Voice That Doesn't Gloat
**Structural summary:** The collector gives the next instruction — courteous, exact, frictionless: which panel, which sequence, the unhurried "when you're ready, Russ." Russ realizes the calm in the earpiece is HIS OWN calm with the love drained out — himself made into a tool. He complies because non-compliance is measured in Donna and the baby.

**Character annotations:**
- **The collector**
  - Subtext: Running a ticket. The courtesy is procedure, not sadism — and that is worse.
  - Unspoken want: Compliance without incident. Stated want: the panel, the sequence, Russ's hands moving.
  - Voice cadence: Exact, unhurried, never gloats. "When you're ready, Russ." Menace lives entirely in courtesy and patience. Cut any smirk, any over-explain.
  - Physical expression: None — render his costlessness as the precise mirror of Russ's cost. Where Russ's calm has a price ticking in his chest, the collector's calm has no body at all.
- **Russ**
  - Subtext: The mirror horror — he is listening to himself on a Tuesday, the same flat helpfulness, with the love subtracted. Recognizing it is the first real wound of the chapter.
  - Unspoken want: To NOT be that voice; to find the seam between them and fail to. Stated want: comply, protect the family, finish.
  - Voice cadence: Flat, compliant, interior dread under it. He answers the collector the way he'd answer a dispatcher. RENDER THE TWO CALMS SIDE BY SIDE IN HIS HEAD — his calm costs, the collector's costs nothing.
  - Physical expression: Hands obey before his stomach does. A swallow. The trained nod to a voice that can't see him nod.

**Relationship dynamic:** The mirror. Hero and antagonist as the same instrument, one with humanity (cost) and one without (frictionless). Power is total and quiet — the collector never raises it because he never has to.
**Dialogue direction:** Collector courteous and minimal. Russ's replies clipped, dispatch-flat. The horror is in the SYMMETRY — let Russ notice they sound alike and have nowhere to put that.

### Beat 3 — Cut to the Perimeter (comedy curdling; amateurs at a live fence)
**Structural summary:** Controlled cut OUT to the team at the perimeter — rendered through Russ's earpiece bleed / open burner so we stay tethered to his POV (he HEARS them, fragmentary). Theo over-explains a plan nobody asked for; Marco issues a numbered list to people physically climbing a fence; Devang notes there's "no API for a chain-link fence." Funny — and terrible, because they're loud and exposed against a professional's running op.

**Character annotations:**
- **Theo**
  - Subtext: Talking to keep from freezing again; every extra word is a hedge against the Ch11 "I can't go."
  - Unspoken want: To be forgiven and useful. Stated want: to brief the plan, to be heard.
  - Voice cadence: Too many words, performing/apologetic, flop-sweat audible in the run-ons. Over-talks a live perimeter.
  - Physical expression: (heard) breath, fumbling, the fence rattling under uncertain hands.
- **Marco** (remote, on chat + burner)
  - Subtext: The archivist weaponizing structure against chaos — a list is the only control he has, and it's useless here.
  - Unspoken want: To be ASSIGNED, to matter in the body of the op, not just the index. Stated want: deliver the steps.
  - Voice cadence: CAPS, numbered lists, em-dash fracture, "stand by" / "ASSIGN ME." Lists now issued to fence-climbers — the comedy of precision aimed at chain-link.
  - Physical expression: (on chat `> marco:` lowercase line; the content CAPS) — render as text bleed.
- **Devang** (remote)
  - Subtext: A systems mind reaching for an interface that the human world refuses to expose.
  - Unspoken want: A clean endpoint. Stated want: to point out the un-routable absurdity.
  - Voice cadence: Acronyms; the curdling laugh-line "no API for a chain-link fence." May land WARM ONCE here — one human beat before words die again.
  - Physical expression: (heard/chat) — flat, dry delivery.
- **Cole** (on-site)
  - Subtext: Already past words; the only one doing instead of narrating.
  - Unspoken want: To witness and act. Stated want: none spoken.
  - Voice cadence: SILENT on burner (RULING #2). Presence relayed by others.
  - Physical expression: (relayed) at the fence, hands working, no talk.
- **Russ** (POV anchor)
  - Subtext: This is the cruelest comfort — his friends, near, alive in his ear, and it sharpens the dread because they're outclassed and he can't warn them without the collector hearing.
  - Unspoken want: For them to be smarter, quieter, gone. Stated want: keep working so the tone doesn't notice his hands stopped.
  - Voice cadence: Interior — he HEARS the comedy and it does not relieve him; it curdles. He might reflexively file their chaos as a "job" too, and hate that he can.
  - Physical expression: A held breath at a laugh that arrives wrong; hands still moving on the panel.

**Relationship dynamic:** The amateurs vs. a professional op they can't see the edges of. Affection sharpens dread. Theo/Cole scar present but not foregrounded yet (loads for Beat 5). Russ tethered to them by sound, severed by the job.
**Dialogue direction:** Fragmentary, overheard, overlapping. Comedy = coping voices + catastrophic under-equipment, heard THROUGH Russ as cruel comfort. Chat lines `> name:` lowercase; burner = spoken prose. The laugh must land and then STOP in the same breath.

### Beat 4 — The Inner Door Opens (target partially seen)
**Structural summary:** Russ completes the sequence; the inner cold-room door releases. He sees what the credential opens — PARTIALLY: rows in the cold, a manifest, a held thing/space telling him this was never about money. Access to PEOPLE-AS-RECORDS / something kept that shouldn't be kept. He grasps the shape without naming it. His low-and-slow flatness wavers — one near-surface crack.

**Character annotations:**
- **Russ**
  - Subtext: The fulcrum from competence to complicity lands in his body. He understands what he is FOR. The make-it-smaller reflex does its most monstrous work here — and it's the only thing keeping him upright.
  - Unspoken want: To not have seen it; to un-know. Stated want (to himself): catalog it as a job — "inventory," "storage," "spec" — so he can keep his hands.
  - Voice cadence: Interior eaten-word at MAXIMUM (RULING #1). The true noun never forms; work-order synonyms arrive in its place and the sentences scan clean. ONE near-surface crack — his version of Donna's SPENT/SEALED — a single line where the flatness fails and re-seals.
  - Physical expression: A stillness that is not calm now but shock wearing calm's clothes. The gloved hand maybe rising halfway and stopping. Breath out of sync with the elapsed tone for the first time.
- **The collector** (in ear)
  - Subtext: None — costless. He notes Russ's pause without alarm.
  - Unspoken want: Russ to keep moving. Stated want: minimal — perhaps the recurring note begins to surface ("We'll correct it together") as pure procedure.
  - Voice cadence: Courteous, frictionless, NO acknowledgment of horror. The flatness against Russ's wavering flatness IS the contrast.
  - Physical expression: None.

**Relationship dynamic:** Russ alone with the consequence; the collector as the voice that will not let him stop to feel it. The cold room is the second silent character — it answers his read from Beat 1 with the worst possible "heat."
**Dialogue direction:** Spare. Show consequence, withhold the noun. Almost no collector line; let the room speak. The gut-punch is interior and quiet — DO NOT over-write it. The monstrosity is that he can still make it smaller.

### Beat 5 — The Moral Vise Loads (save him ≠ stop the job)
**Structural summary:** Through the burner bleed, Russ hears the team commit to BREACH — coming IN for him. He understands the trap geometry before they do: the only way they reach him is THROUGH the door he opened; pulling him out COMPLETES the access; stopping the job means leaving him in. The collector confirms it without cruelty — "They're helping, Russ. Let them." Russ tries to warn them off — weaponizing the eaten-word, this time deliberately, to hide info from the listening collector — and isn't sure they'll parse it in time.

**Character annotations:**
- **Russ**
  - Subtext: The unbearable math of love. To be saved is to be completed-as-the-tool. He cannot say so plainly because the collector hears everything.
  - Unspoken want: To stop them WITHOUT the collector understanding he's stopping them. Stated want (on burner, coded): something that sounds like coordinates/instruction but EATS the operative word.
  - Voice cadence: Burner eaten-word — VOLUNTARY and NOTICED (the distinction in RULING #1). Callback to "out past Syl—". He chooses the swallow this time; we feel him choose it. Flat enough to pass the collector, broken enough that maybe Donna reads cold and catches it.
  - Physical expression: The held breath; a hand pressed flat to the cold (Beat 1 rhyme pre-echo); jaw tight on the word he won't finish.
- **The collector**
  - Subtext: He has already won the geometry; he doesn't need to threaten.
  - Unspoken want: Russ to let the rescue complete the job. Stated want: "They're helping, Russ. Let them." — courteous, no cruelty, no gloat.
  - Voice cadence: Frictionless, the recurring "We'll correct it together" available. Render his costless calm against Russ's calm now visibly bleeding cost.
  - Physical expression: None.
- **Donna** (relayed, ONE line)
  - Subtext: She reads cold; she may be the only one who catches Russ's swallowed word.
  - Unspoken want: Russ alive AND her people out — and she knows she may not get both. Stated want: hold the team, make the call.
  - Voice cadence: Dry steel, minimum volume, foreman. Her "you don't get to die for me twice" ENERGY without repeating the line. The ONE near-surface crack that re-seals — budget it HERE or Beat 4, not both surfaces fully.
  - Physical expression: (relayed — off-page) voice only, flat and cold.
- **Theo / Cole** (perimeter)
  - Subtext: The scar, re-tested under live stakes.
  - Unspoken want: Theo — to be the one who gets through this time, not the one who froze. Cole — to do the next thing.
  - Voice cadence: Theo over-talks the breach; Cole silent. The WORDLESS object/keycard hand-off (RULING #2) is the thing that actually gets them through. WORKS, NOT reconciled.
  - Physical expression: Theo's hands fumbling, Cole's hands sure; the hand-off as the functional sentence.

**Relationship dynamic:** Love as a trap spring. The team's rescue and the job's completion are the SAME motion — the cruelest geometry in the book. Russ vs. collector now a real contest, fought in what Russ can hide. Theo/Cole functional, unhealed.
**Dialogue direction:** Russ's coded burner line carrying a deliberately eaten word — tension on whether they parse it. Collector courteous and certain. Donna ONE relayed line of cold steel. Theo's talk over Cole's silence; the hand-off lands wordless.

### Beat 6 — The Hand on the Man (close rhyme + escalated mid-word snatch)
**Structural summary:** The breach lands. Russ's hand — the same hand that read the cold door in Beat 1 — comes to rest on a MAN (the kept thing/person, or the first teammate through, or Russ himself being moved). Hand on a door became hand on a man. In the same breath the collector finally NAMES the target — the first hard reveal — and the word is RUSS-EATEN: the job itself snatches him off the reveal as the door does its work, the vise snaps (saving = completing), and the chapter cuts on the half-formed target word. The last beat IS the snatch.

**Character annotations:**
- **Russ**
  - Subtext: Vertigo. He almost knows the whole shape and it's taken at the lip. The hand that was a technician's is now on a person — competence consummated as complicity.
  - Unspoken want: To finish hearing the word — to KNOW — and to be spared knowing. Both. Stated want: nothing speakable; he is being moved by the job.
  - Voice cadence: Interior, narrowing to almost nothing. The PERSON-eaten device: the collector's naming begins and the JOB eats it — not a typo, the door doing its work pulls Russ off the reveal mid-word. End on the broken word. No resolution.
  - Physical expression: Hand on the man — flat palm, the Beat 1 gesture transposed. Reading a body for heat the way he read the door. The rhyme must land HARD.
- **The collector**
  - Subtext: Closing the ticket; the naming is procedure, not triumph.
  - Unspoken want: The job complete. Stated want: the first hard name of the target — courteous, frictionless, the recurring note possibly folded in ("We'll correct it together" / the name).
  - Voice cadence: Even, unhurried — and the JOB cuts him off in Russ's perception, not the collector faltering. The word half-forms and is gone.
  - Physical expression: None.
- **Teammate through the breach** (at least one in-room for the first time)
  - Subtext: Whoever it is, they are the rescue that is also the completion. If it's Cole — wordless, a hand. If Theo — too many words at the worst moment.
  - Unspoken want: Get Russ out. Stated want: same.
  - Voice cadence: If Cole — silent/object. If Theo — a clipped, breaking line, finally too few words.
  - Physical expression: The breach body, the reaching hand, the motion that completes the door.

**Relationship dynamic:** Open/close rhyme consummated — hand/door → hand/man. The savior and the saw-blade are one person walking through one door. The collector's voice and the job's machinery become the same eraser, taking the word at the lip.
**Dialogue direction:** Collector begins the hard naming; the JOB eats it. PERSON-eaten, escalated from line-eaten (Ch11). Cut on the half-formed target word. No closure — the door has turned again. The very last beat IS the snatch.
