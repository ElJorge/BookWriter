**Structural summary:** Continuous from Ch17. Light dies; the refrigeration hum dies a half-second later — the SILENCE is the first wrong thing. **Character annotations:**
- **RUSS**
  - Subtext: Two competencies stripped in one second — he reads hands (now invisible) and he survives by saying nothing (now there's no face to read his nothing FROM).   - Subtext: She's not soothing him; she's compensating. With no face to read, she does the only legible thing — she states the room. Her plainness is COMPETENCE here, not chatter; she omits nothing because in the dark there's no one to protect and protection-by-omission is useless anyway.

**Relationship dynamic:** First contact of the two-hander, established as a SOUND relationship — he can't see her, she can't read him; both reduced to voice. The collector-target and the man-who-opens-doors, alone in the only room that matters.
**Dialogue direction:** Open on Rhea mid-inventory (the blackout ate the front of her sentence — first taste of "world eats the word").
