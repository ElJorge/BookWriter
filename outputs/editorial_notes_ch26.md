### Fictive-frame / device-naming scrub (before → after)

1. **Beat 6 — cinematographic frame + structural-payoff naming (the only real breach).**
   - Before: *"That had changed. She rendered it to herself as a wide shot and not a close one, because his face was not the resolution and never could be. The resolution was the count of grips on the steel beside his."*
   - After: *"That had changed. She did not look at his face — his face was not the thing that had changed. The thing that had changed was the count of grips on the steel beside his."*
   - Reason: "rendered it to herself as a wide shot / close one" is camera-language framing the prose as composed; "the resolution was…" names a structural payoff. Both converted to Rhea's in-world looking-and-counting.

2. **Beat 9 — narrator naming the echo (the close-rhyme risk flagged in the brief).**
   - Before: *"The same act of counting she had begun at the door, and the weight of it had turned all the way over inside her while the number held still."*
   - After: *"She had counted at the door too. The number was the same size it had been then. The weight of it was not."*
   - Reason: "the same act of counting" + "turned all the way over" edges toward the narrator explaining the open/close echo as a device. Recast so Rhea simply counts again and registers the changed weight; the rhyme lands through her perception, unnamed.

3. **Beat 5 — gloss naming the fact as a constructed double-meaning.**
   - Before: *"She turned the fact over and found it was two facts wearing one word. Being un-erasable was the protection. Being un-erasable was the sentence."*
   - After: *"Being un-erasable was the protection. Being un-erasable was the sentence. Same word, both times."*
   - Reason: "two facts wearing one word" is a writerly meta-frame on the wordplay; trimmed to the bare doubling, which is more Rhea (plain, flat, lets the repetition do it).
