## Assumptions

- The final Donna-perceived image: door left open a half-inch AND the count set down whole — outline offers a choice; both are small enough to coexist in the closing beat without crowding. Prose stage may keep one if space is tight; door-at-a-half-inch is the inviolable last image candidate.

### Beat 1 — THE HEADCOUNT THAT CAME BACK WRONG (open the rhyme; both registers immediately)
**Structural summary:** Cold open. Donna in a borrowed room, counting heads — the thing she does first, always. For years the count came back one short. This morning it's off the *other* way: too many. Rhea's here. Theo brought too much coffee. She recounts twice, annoyed. Everyone's marked now; nobody can disappear.

**Character annotations:**
- **DONNA**
  - Subtext: A foreman who can't get a clean count has a problem — and for the first time the problem is surplus, not loss. She doesn't trust it. She's spent years bracing for one-short; her reflex can't parse one-over. The annoyance is grief's muscle memory firing in the wrong direction.
  - Unspoken want: For the count to be *right* — which she has always meant as "nobody missing." She does not yet have a word for wanting "everybody here, and then some."
  - Voice cadence: CUE-voice. A number called flat. "Count." A name. Recount commands clipped, dry. Do NOT let her narrate the feeling — she clocks the surplus and recounts; the prose carries the warmth she won't.
  - Physical expression: Eyes do the count, not a clipboard — she counts people the way she reads hands, by face. A small stall when the number won't sit. Maybe a second pass with a finger, the foreman's tell that something's off.
- **THEO**
  - Subtext: Brought coffee for more people than the crew because he's already counting the room the way Donna does — surplus as hope. He doesn't say it.
  - Voice cadence: Already half-talking, warming up; not yet the connector-gift, just texture. Comedy ballast.
  - Physical expression: Too many cups. The over-provision is the joke and the love.
- **RHEA**
  - Subtext: Being *in the count* at all is the whole arc landing quietly. She's present, not processed.
  - Physical expression: Just there, taking up a place — a body Donna didn't deploy.

**Relationship dynamic:** Donna/room — foreman to crew, but the authority is recalibrating in real time; she commands a room that's larger than her roster and doesn't yet know the cues for that. Mirror-plant: she reads the room true (collector's opposite) even while annoyed.
**Dialogue direction:** Open on a CUE and a number. Land the recount as comedy with grief underneath. ONE tell-don't-show max for the marked-state (per outline) — oblique, the column they chose. Both registers in the first 200 words. Do NOT name the rhyme; let the wrong-direction count be felt.
