## Annotated Beats

### Beat 1 — THE BLANK FIELD (open; Marco intakes Devang's thread)
**Structural summary:** Marco takes Devang's Ch19 trace-dump into the archive and processes it by METHOD — alphabetizing dread, numbering panic, cross-reffing the unknown to make it hold still. He logs the dump as a new card, hits a BLANK FIELD, and we learn his lifelong axiom: a blank field means "pending / not yet catalogued," NEVER "gone." Comedy: a man who needs a noun for his killer so he can file it. Horror seeded: the dump is too CLEAN — like something already swept it.

**Character annotations:**
- **MARCO** (POV) — *Mode: REMOTE text to Devang (`> marco:`) for the handoff ack; INTERIOR + ALOUD muttering to himself as he files (prose-in-quotes for the spoken half-sentences he says to the empty room).*
  - Subtext: Filing is not bureaucracy for Marco — it's how he breathes through terror. Cataloging the thing that wants to kill him is the only way he can look at it. He is calm the way a man is calm laying out instruments before surgery on himself.
  - Unspoken want: To make the unknown HOLD STILL long enough to survive it. (Stated want: "log the dump, get a clean intake.")
  - Voice cadence: Hot from line one. CAPS for the field-labels he types: SOURCE—. ORIGIN—. THREAT [class]—. A numbered intake list that runs smooth: "1. mirror it. 2. timestamp it. 3. name it —" and then 3 can't finish, the dash opening. Em-dash where the noun should be.
  - Physical expression: Hands move faster than dread — index cards squared to the edge, drawer opening with a thumb-click he's done ten thousand times. He smooths a card flat like petting an animal. The TELL: he keeps glancing at the dump's metadata and not finding a fingerprint, and his pen stops.
  - Comedy/dread duet: COMEDY — he genuinely tries to alphabetize an existential threat, wants a NOUN so it fits the catalog ("everything fits SOMEWHERE"). DREAD — the dump is too clean; "swept" is the word that floats up and he files it under a heading he doesn't like.
- **DEVANG** — *Mode: REMOTE (`> devang:`) — dry handoff.*
  - Subtext: Devang has done his part and is shoving the mess to the one man who keeps mess. Dry to the point of cold.
  - Voice cadence: Clipped, no comfort. `> devang: it's yours. it's clean. too clean. tell me why.` — he frames it as a SPEC problem, not a fear.
- **DONNA** — *Mode: LIVE, one cue (earpiece, prose-in-quotes).*
  - Subtext: She's checking the room without crowding it. One cue, trusting Marco to work.
  - Voice cadence: "Talk when you have a shape, Marco." — permission to take his time, which is also pressure.

**Relationship dynamic:** Devang → Marco is a clean handoff between two specialists who don't need to be warm to trust each other; the "too clean" is the seed Devang plants and Marco will grow. Donna hovers light.
**Dialogue direction:** Establish the AXIOM out loud or in CAPS so the close can invert it: a blank field = "not yet catalogued," a benign, hopeful thing. Let Marco SAY it (prose-in-quotes muttered) or type it (CAPS card). End the beat on the dump's missing fingerprint stopping his pen — comedy of order meeting the first cold draft.
