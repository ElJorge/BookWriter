The word pulls him off the buckle. He doesn't stand yet. But the hand on the door changes pressure.

### Beat 4 — THE ERASURE STOPPED (the scheduled deletion fails)
The forward-dated erasure with the crew's shape — the deletion the engine had queued — tries to execute and can't: to erase the column it would have to process the refusal first, and it's still gagging on it; the record it would have to overwrite is now in too many hands. ### Beat 5 — THE COLLECTOR'S TRUE NATURE (Donna reads him true)
DONNA (thesis-holder / the mirror) NAMES his nature — not a face, a moral shape. There IS a human behind it: the recurring fingerprint, the architect. His nature: THE MAN OF TOTAL OMISSION. He built the engine so NO hand — including his own — is ever on anything; his whole power was never having a hand to grab, never a word on record, never a choice anyone could point to. He is the exact inverse of the chain: total disclosure / many hands / a refusal said aloud vs total omission / no hands / nothing ever said. Keep him OUTSIDE-ONLY — glimpsed, identified, located (a screen, a routing path, a place), never a gloating face-to-face; frictionless to the end, no speech. Tie to RUSS directly: Russ's eraser is INVOLUNTARY (trauma eats his words); the collector's omission is DELIBERATE (he eats his own hand from every act). Russ recognizes the thing that spent him — not a monster, a method; the instrument recognizing the man who never touched the instrument.

### Beat 6 — COLE'S AFTERMATH (the seal broken, the man unchanged)
Return to Cole — wordless again. The single word was the payoff; do not give him a speech. Optionally one second minimal utterance ONLY if devastatingly earned — default: wordless, the door of him shut again, the cost of the open written on him.

### Beat 7 — THE COST / RUSS'S ARC RESOLVES (company, not erasure)
Pay the price. The win worked BECAUSE they're all exposed now — submitting the chained record made every crew member permanently MARKED; each chose the column; erase one = erase all. The chain now SHARES Russ's brand. He has been, since Ch14, the man who opened the door — rescue won, exoneration LOST, isolated in the mark. That isolation ENDS here — not by erasing the brand (exoneration is still lost; that NEVER comes back) but by COMPANY: everyone chose the same mark, on purpose, together. Russ feels it land — the handler who was handled, the instrument the collector spent, is now a hand among hands. Here he says a DIFFERENT true thing he never could — NOT the cold-room noun (never), but his own complicity / a refusal of his own / a name that isn't the cold-room synonym — paralleling Cole's word WITHOUT repeating it. He completes a sentence for once. Keep the gallows live: maybe what he finally says is rueful, the worst-timed honesty, the wrong joke at the right moment. Optionally compound with a physical/tactical cost so it isn't clean.
