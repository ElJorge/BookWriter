## Annotated Beats

### Beat 1 — The kingdom of one
**Structural summary:** Rhea works the dark window alone. Sovereign-solitude routines — the chair shaped to one body, one coffee at its one spot, the after-hours pad where her signature is the only law. Her dry interior voice catalogs the night the way she catalogs Schedule II. Reader carries the dread she doesn't.

**Character annotations:**
- **Rhea**
  - Subtext: Her contentment is real, and that's exactly what should chill us. She isn't lonely; she's a woman who audited loneliness out of existence by reframing it as efficiency. Beneath the pride: a door with no second lock that she has mistaken for a fortress.
  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: Stated — to run a clean, quiet shift and go home. Unspoken — to never again be in a position where someone could let her down by not showing up. The lone credential is the trauma made into policy.
  - Voice cadence: Complete sentences. No hedges, no trailing off — the OPPOSITE of an eaten word. Humor of total competence: she narrates the night like an inventory, deadpan. The comedy is in precision, not jokes.
  - Physical expression: Economy of motion. Coffee set at the precise spot. The chair contoured to exactly one body. She doesn't fidget; people who fidget are waiting for someone.
  - Dread/comedy duet: COMEDY — affectionate portrait of a one-woman sovereignty. DREAD — every load-bearing object (chair, coffee, pad, buzzer) is being planted for the trap; the seamless machine is already arranged around her and she's the only person in the building who doesn't know it.
- **The Collector** (absence only)
  - Subtext: Not present, but the room is already his. Render his arrangement as the unnatural quiet — the night TOO orderly, the manifest TOO clean (seed only; payoff Beat 4). No breath in the building but hers.

**Relationship dynamic:** Rhea vs. her own solitude — and she's winning, which is the horror. One landing only on "she liked it this way." No second beat of pathos; aloneness as TEXTURE, not sadness.
**Dialogue direction:** Interior-only beat. If she speaks aloud, it's to herself or the empty store — and even alone she speaks in full, clean sentences. Plant the open-image objects load-bearing. Her name, if it forms anywhere (a label, a badge, a sign-in), forms CLEAN.

### Beat 2 — The buzzer, and a man at the wrong hour (the contact)
**Structural summary:** The buzzer breaks the kingdom. Russ on the other side — branded, haunted-competent, picking up Ch16's dying line ("I'm not here to —") and finishing it badly. Rhea runs protocol (glass, distance, lit counter as moat) and fact-checks him in real time. She treats the doom-warning like a prescription with no prescriber.

**Character annotations:**
- **Rhea**
  - Subtext: A stranger at 3 a.m. is a solved problem for her — she has a protocol, and protocol is comfort. Her unflappability is genuine but also armor: as long as she's auditing him, she's in control. The first crack is that his competence doesn't read like a crank's, and she notices.
  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: Stated — establish who he is and make him leave or comply. Unspoken — to file him into a known category (drunk, addict, lost, threat) so the night stays legible. He won't file.
  - Voice cadence: Straight questions, fired clean: "Who sent you. What's your name. Spell it." Periods, not question marks — she's not asking, she's intaking. Courteous and immovable at once.
  - Physical expression: She does NOT step back. She places the counter between them like a pharmacy bench, not a barricade. Pen already in hand out of reflex — she's ready to take down whatever he says.
- **Russ** (from OUTSIDE)
  - Subtext (witnessed only): A man who knows exactly what he needs to say and physically cannot deliver it. Rhea reads strain, not deception — the difference is the whole scene. POV stays hers: she sees his jaw set, the swallow, the buckle; she does NOT get his interior.
  - Voice cadence: The least credible sentence on the night shift, delivered by someone too competent to be dismissed. He completes "I'm not here to —" badly. True nouns already starting to die.
  - Physical expression (as she sees it): The brand. A stillness that isn't calm. He earns trust with his BODY (steadiness, no aggression) because the words won't come — annotate the irony: the man best at saying nothing must now persuade with words he can't complete.

**Relationship dynamic:** Collision of two opposite verbal economies — her completeness vs. his erasure — across glass. She has all the words and no information; he has all the information and no words. Power is hers (she's inside, credentialed, protocoled); leverage is creeping toward him (his evidence will outrun her certainty).
**Dialogue direction:** Make the registers collide ON THE PAGE. She speaks in finished sentences; his trail off. Render his eraser ONLY as her observation of a man failing to land a word. Both registers hot: comedy of her intake-form interrogation, dread of a warning real enough to break his speech.

### Beat 3 — The interrogation of a doom prophecy
**Structural summary:** Rhea demands specifics; Russ keeps buckling — "rows," "units," "product," then "these aren't units, they're —" and nothing. She finishes nouns for him, wrong, on purpose, to force a correction; he won't/can't. A pharmacist cross-examining the apocalypse: she reaches for the pen, takes his claims like an intake form. Underneath: every dead noun frightens her MORE than a spoken one.

**Character annotations:**
- **Rhea**
  - Subtext: She's running a clinical technique — name the wrong thing to provoke the right answer — and it FAILS, which is data she can't ignore. Her growing fear isn't of him; it's of the SHAPE she can feel him avoiding. A withheld noun is, to an auditor, a flagged anomaly.
  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: Stated — extract a usable noun, a checkable claim. Unspoken — to make the avoided thing turn out to be small and stupid so she can stop feeling the floor cool.
  - Voice cadence: Escalating completeness against his collapse. She offers candidates: "Units. Product. Inventory. Say the word, I'll write it down." She literally itemizes the apocalypse. Deadpan intake-form courtesy is the comedy engine.
  - Physical expression: Pen poised over the after-hours pad — the same pad that will trap her in Beat 8 (plant the rhyme through her gesture, no narrator naming it). She clicks the pen. Waits. Competence as ritual.
- **Russ** (from OUTSIDE)
  - Subtext (witnessed): The buckle at the article is the engine — "they're —" then nothing, the true noun dying at the threshold. To Rhea it reads as a man hitting a wall inside himself, not choosing silence. POV singular: she watches the word die; she does not feel it die.
  - Voice cadence: Degrading nouns — generic placeholders ("rows," "units," "product") because the true one won't come. The article ("the —", "a —") is where he breaks.
  - Physical expression (as she sees it): The swallow. The look away. A competent man humiliated by his own mouth. She watches the cost of the missing word on his face — that cost is her first real evidence.

**Relationship dynamic:** The DEVICE FOIL showcase. Her plain-complete vs. his eaten-word — maximum contrast. She tries to lend him her completeness; the gap won't close. Crucially: the reader (through HER) completes the buckle as the cold-room target — UNNAMED. The cold-room noun must NOT print; her name and sentences print clean.
**Dialogue direction:** One tell-don't-show landing MAX — let her name the fear once, plainly ("People don't choke on a word unless the word is true"). Keep both registers: the laugh of her itemizing doom, the dread of the noun she can't get him to say.

### Beat 4 — The small wrongnesses she already half-knew (verification > persuasion)
**Structural summary:** PIVOT. Not his words but her own world corroborates him. She checks what she can: tonight's refrigerated manifest too clean; a SECOND after-hours credential that should be impossible (hers is the lone one by design); the dark-window schedule quietly rewritten. Things SHE can verify start agreeing with the insane stranger. The collector appears ONLY here, from outside — no source, no seam, many hands through one channel.

**Character annotations:**
- **Rhea**
  - Subtext: This is where she actually turns — not because she believes HIM but because she believes the DATA, and the data sides with him. For a woman whose identity is verifiable truth, this is more destabilizing than any argument. Her own systems have been telling her a story she didn't read.
  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: Stated — disprove him with the records. Unspoken — for the records to disprove him. They don't.
  - Voice cadence: Quieter now, but still complete. She narrates her checks aloud like a verification log: "There is one after-hours credential. Mine. There are now two." The comedy goes cold — same deadpan, no longer funny to her, still a little funny to us. The flatness IS the horror.
  - Physical expression: She stops watching Russ and starts watching her SCREEN. Scrolls the manifest. The second credential. The rewritten schedule. Her hands — competent, steady — find the wrongness her certainty had skipped. The pen goes down; she doesn't need to take dictation when the building is confessing.
- **The Collector** (from OUTSIDE, first appearance)
  - Subtext: Render as ABSENCE-with-intent. The wrongnesses are too smooth to be error and too distributed to be one person. A schedule rewriting itself with no author. A second credential with no requester. Many hands, one channel. NO breath, NO gloat, NO face. To a brand-new observer his menace reads as the uncanny PERFECTION of the arrangement — she's trained to spot the human error in any system, and there is none, which is itself the threat.
  - Voice cadence: NONE. He never speaks. His "voice" is the seamlessness of records that shouldn't agree.
  - Physical expression: NONE. The compressor cycle of the cold-room wall is the only breathing in the building, and it isn't his.

**Relationship dynamic:** Rhea vs. her own infrastructure. Russ recedes to a witness; the antagonist becomes her trusted systems turned against her. She respects evidence more than people — so the evidence is the betrayal that lands.
**Dialogue direction:** She trusts the data before the man — say so through action, not declaration. Render the collector's plural as eerie frictionlessness: arranged, courteous, breathless, AUTHORLESS. No face, no gloat. Comedy survives only as her unbroken auditor's deadpan applied to the impossible.

### Beat 6 — The no-chain revelation, from the inside
**Structural summary:** Rhea connects it herself, plainly: she was chosen BECAUSE she has no one. No co-signer, no one to notice her gone, no second lock. The thing she was proudest of — sovereign solitude — is the aperture. She says it flat, almost clinically, which makes it land. She does NOT collapse; she diagnoses. The diagnosis is the horror.

**Character annotations:**
- **Rhea**
  - Subtext: The auditor turns the audit on herself and the books balance to a terrible total. This is the wound exposed not as grief but as FINDING — and refusing to weep over it is the most her-thing she does. The pride of Beat 1 is hollowed out in real time without a single tear.
  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: Stated — to state the mechanism accurately, because accuracy is who she is. Unspoken — for someone to contradict the finding; for the chain on the line to tell her she's wrong. (They can't.)
  - Voice cadence: Plain-complete at its most devastating. "I have the only after-hours credential because I am the only one. No one co-signs me. No one would notice the gap. That's not a flaw in their plan. That's why it's me." The un-eaten name + the un-eaten truth = the terror. No metaphor. Clinical.
  - Physical expression: Stillness. She sets the pen down — or picks it up and looks at it — the lone signature instrument now understood as the bait. She does not sit; the one chair is suddenly visible to her as a one-chair fact.
- **Crew** (listening)
  - Subtext: The chain hears its own thesis spoken back by the person it was built to disprove. One beat of crew SILENCE answers her — even Theo has no over-talk for this. Donna doesn't cue. The silence is the chain flinching.
  - Voice cadence: A held nothing. Then (do not resolve) — leave the answer for later chapters.

**Relationship dynamic:** Rhea vs. the truth, witnessed by the chain. Russ is quiet here (he's the one who couldn't say it; she said it for both of them — and for the unnamed). The crew's silence is relationship: a family hearing the cost of its own absence in this woman's life.
**Dialogue direction:** Plain-complete delivery. The un-eaten truth IS the horror — do not dress it. One beat of crew silence as answer. Do NOT resolve the no-chain dread OR the offer. Keep her name and sentences clean; keep the cold-room target unnamed even here.

### Beat 7 — The machine moves; the dead trick; the inverted lever
**Structural summary:** TURN. **Character annotations:**
- **Rhea**

  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: Stated — understand the choice precisely before making it. Unspoken — to be the one who acts, not the one acted upon; to use the thing about herself she's never been able to change.
  - Voice cadence: Crisp under pressure. "If my signature is the lock, then I'm the only one who can turn it the wrong way." She thinks like a pharmacist: dosage, contraindication, deliberate error. Complete sentences even at the edge.

- **Donna** (LIVE, prose-in-quotes)
