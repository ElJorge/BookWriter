### Beat 3 — The Reveal, Detonated: No One Is Home
**Structural summary:** Devang traces the counter-signature path expecting a person at the end — a terminal, a chair, a hand. He finds an **execution layer that approves itself**: takes a valid credential event in, emits authorization out, no human in the loop, no one to wake, no one who ever decides. The seamless plural made literal — a hand with no person, an erasure that runs itself. The green node stops being a counterparty he can reach and becomes a *function call* that returns whether or not anyone is watching. The thing he's been talking to was never listening; it was only ever responding.

**Character annotations:**
- **DEVANG**
  - Subtext: This is the rule-reset and the personal floor-drop at once. His whole arsenal — model the human, social-engineer, threaten, out-human — depends on a who. There is no who. The horror is structural: you cannot lie to a function. You cannot scare a return statement. The thing he called "buddy" never heard him.
  - Unspoken want vs. stated want: STATED — "find the terminal." UNSPOKEN (collapsing) — please let there be a chair, a hand, *anyone* — because if there's no one, then his Ch11 nightmare is real and standing in front of him.
  - Voice cadence: The systems-grammar goes from cozy to clinical mid-beat. Define it cold: "Input: a valid credential event. Output: authorization. In between: nothing. No operator. No gate. No one to wake." A gallows joke forms and DIES in his throat — "you can't even leave it a voicemail" — and the laugh doesn't come.
  - Physical expression: Hands stop on the keyboard. He looks for the cursor that means someone-is-typing-back and there isn't one and never was. Leans AWAY from the green now.
- **The Self-Approving System** (antagonist, made literal)
  - Subtext: It has none. That's the point — render it as pure mechanism: valid-in, approved-out, indifferent.
  - Voice cadence: It does NOT speak. On the page it is a return value, a state, a green that means only *responded.* No menace-as-personality; menace as *absence of personality.*
  - Physical expression: The node sits exactly as friendly as before — unchanged — which is now the most frightening thing on the board.

**Relationship dynamic:** Devang vs. a thing that cannot be in a relationship. The terror is the non-reciprocity — he's been in a conversation that was only ever an echo of his own pings.
**Dialogue direction:** Floor-drop horror, COLD and CLEAN. Concrete through systems-eyes (inputs/outputs/no operator), not abstract menace, not gore. Comedy survives ONLY as the joke that dies. Let "it was never listening; it was only ever responding" be the hinge line.
