## Assumptions

- **Cole's execution voice (answering Q1):** I lock his standing register HARD — near-mute, observational, un-narrating — and vector it from witnessing to DOING **without adding eloquence or word-count.** The turn is rendered as a *change in the verbs, not the volume*: the courier who read packages now reads routes, vehicles, places, scenes, and finally the man — same clean instrument, new object. The doing IS his feeling: where another POV would ruminate, Cole *acts* or *files a fact*. **I deliberately keep his interiority to (a) clipped perception, (b) the occasional two-or-three-word italic fact (the Ch8 grammar: "*Seat warm. Engine running.*"), and (c) at most one un-tidied fragment per beat that carries the emotional load by what it WON'T say.** Where the seeing risks tipping novelistic, I pull back to pure courier-observation (the lock on his sparseness is more important than any beautiful line). This is the chapter's hardest craft call and I flag it at every beat below.
- **The administration seen, lock held (answering Q2):** Cole SEES Russ used — externally only. I render it as a courier reading equipment: a working man made into a tool, *observed as physical fact*, never as Russ's interior thought/feeling. The devastation comes from the cleanness of the observation, not from access to Russ's head. If a line risks entering Russ, it pulls back to surface (posture, hands, the trade in motion, the courteous direction over the top).
- **The collector's noticing — small-sign-first (answering Q2's flagged choice):** I take the architect's lean: the noticing is a SMALL chilling external sign in Beat 5 (a thing moved / a light / a contact that shouldn't exist / the magnet's name clocked), with the overt courtesy arriving AS/with the snatch in Beat 6. The counter-move reads like a man updating a ticket — processing a variable he didn't model, not alarmed, just *adjusting*. The courtesy turned toward THEM is the worst thing.
- **Comedy inside the op (the survival of the funny):** The comedy in a tense infiltration survives by living in the *distributed logistics and the coping registers*, never in Cole and never by deflating the dread. Marco's runbook, Devang's route-physics, Theo's bartender-improv, and Donna's minimum-volume command run ON THE SAME BEAT as the dread — the funny and the frightening are the same fact (an HVAC heist run by a group text / a professional turning his head). Cole is the still point: the comedy is AROUND him, his silence the eye of the storm.
- **The scar leaned-on, not healed (answering Q1/Q3):** Cole-and-Theo cooperate on the irreversible thing because the stakes FORCE it, not because it's reconciled. The wound lives IN the cooperation (a hand-off, a glance, a thing not said while they do the thing). I flag any moment that risks over-resolving it — the trust is forced, never earned by a healing.
- **The eaten-word / proof-of-life device:** If Russ gets one more dispensed/eaten-word contact in Ch9, it stays externally observed (heard/seen, never his interior) and the device carries a tell. I assume Beat 5/6 may surface a fragment of Russ's voice or a near-touch contact; I annotate for it but defer the exact mechanism to scene_weaver (it's a structural-rendering call). My lock: whatever Russ does, we never enter his head.
- **`> name: message` grammar:** On-page chat is blockquoted lowercase; live phone / in-person / voice-in-the-ear is spoken prose. Russ's empty green dot is still the wound in the thread.
- **The collector's voice "arriving where the team is" (Beat 6):** I assume the snatch's courtesy may land as the collector's voice reaching the on-site team (not only Donna's landline) — the demonstration that he sees them. I annotate the register; the exact channel (a phone in the place, a voice in the dark, the dispatch app) is scene_weaver's call.

### Beat 1 — The Wheel Moving (the Hands That Drive)

**Structural summary:** Continuous from Ch8's last image — the van on the on-ramp toward Sylmar. Open on COLE: hands at ten-and-two on the missing man's wheel (still in Ch7 → empty in Ch8 → now MOVING), the HAYES HEATING & AIR magnet lit-and-unlit by oncoming headlights, the dome light still dead, the bulb rolling in the cup holder. The thread fires (Marco's plan loading); Donna's command-node forms back at the room. The op is GOING.

**Character annotations:**

- **Cole** *(POV — the spine igniting)*
  - **Subtext:** He is not nervous; he is *occupied*. The wheel under his hands is the missing man's, and he knows it (the warmth, the seat-shape, the bulb someone pulled) — but he does not eulogize it, he reads it. The grief is in the *files he keeps* (the pulled bulb, the name on the door), not in any stated sorrow. He has been the one who finishes deliveries his whole life; tonight the package is a man, and he has decided that doesn't change the job — which is the only way a man like Cole can survive the job.
  - **Unspoken want:** To get there and finish it — to be, for once, the one who *did*, not the one who watched. (Stated want, if anything: "got it" / route confirmations. He does not announce wanting.)
  - **Voice cadence:** SPARE — this is the chapter's load-bearing craft lock. Clipped perception, two-or-three-word italic facts (Ch8 grammar carried: "*Seat warm. Engine running.*" → here "*Magnet on. Bulb rolling. Headlights, then dark, then headlights.*"). He reads the road like a route. NO rumination on "what it means" to drive a dead man's van toward the dead man — he just notes the magnet goes lit/unlit and drives. **Flag to scene_weaver:** resist giving Cole a thesis-sentence here; let the wheel-that-was-still-now-moving be felt through his HANDS (a grip, a gear change), not narrated.

- **Theo** *(passenger — the bartender shotgun)*

  - **Unspoken want:** To be useful and forgiven without having to ask for either. To not be the one who gets them caught.
  - **Voice cadence:** If he speaks, it spills — but here, early, he's compressing it (a single half-sentence that almost spills and then doesn't, because Cole's silence shames the flood into a trickle). The comedy is the dammed flood; the dread is what it's dammed against.
  - **Physical expression:** Hands looking for a job (the Ch8 hovering palm, carried) — adjusts the dispatch phone, touches the door, doesn't know where to put them in a moving van. The bartender's hands with no bar.

- **Devang** *(voice in the ear — the cold map become live routing)*
  - **Subtext:** Vindication and terror fused. "No API for a man in a parking lot" has fully inverted — he IS the API now — and the thing he wanted (to reach the man with his tools) is happening because the man is in danger. He routes to keep from feeling that.
  - **Unspoken want:** To route them true — to be, for once, the map that lands.
  - **Voice cadence:** Turn-by-turn, route-physics narrated into a courier's ear — jargon-warm, the system-diagram become navigation ("in point six, the 210 east, stay right — there's an exhaust on every parking lot and you're about to be on the one with his"). Spoken prose (live in the ear), not blockquote. The cold seeing doing live work.
  - **Physical expression:** (remote — rendered as voice only) the precision of a man reading a screen 400 miles off as if his hands were on the wheel.

- **Marco** *(thread — the plan loading)*
  - **Subtext:** The Sev1 has started; he is in his element and his element is, for once, *for* someone.
  - **Voice cadence:** CAPS, numbered list opening ("1. COPY. 2. PLAN LOADING—"), em-dash fracture. Blockquote grammar.

- **Donna** *(off/forming — the command-node)*
  - **Subtext:** She has just SENT them. The hardest thing she's done — and she can't take it back. Her forming-up is the controller steadying for a long shift she can't walk out of.
  - **Voice cadence:** (minimal here — she's establishing the net) dry, two-word, sealed.

**Relationship dynamic:** Cole/Theo — the scar in the cab from frame one, rendered by what they DON'T say (two men in a small warm space, the knife between them, both looking forward). Cole/Devang — the wordless executor and the voice-in-the-ear: a perfect pairing (one needs no words, one supplies all the words), the comedy of a man who never talks taking turn-by-turn from a man who only talks in topology. The convergence begins: the strands are already feeding Cole's hands.

**Dialogue direction:** Open the chapter's voice-collision immediately — Devang's route-physics (spoken) over Cole's two-word reads (italic/clipped), Theo's almost-flood compressed beside them. ### Beat 2 — The Op Goes Live (the Heist Run by the Group Chat)

Theo gets his cover-improv assignment. The op is GOOD-ENOUGH — the new register, planted to pay later (good-enough is what makes the professional turn his head).

**Character annotations:**

- **Marco** *(remote — the mission-plan-as-runbook)*

He manages the terror by *numbering* it. The list is how he loves.

  - **Voice cadence:** CAPS, numbered lists, em-dash fracture, "EXECUTE STEP 3." The Sev1 register at operational peak — an incident commander running a heist like an outage. Blockquote grammar. The comedy: a man treating a kidnapping like a runbook; the dread: the runbook is for a person.

- **Devang** *(remote — the live route)*
  - **Subtext:** Same as Beat 1, deepening — the wall fully inverted, the dread that the one variable he can't model (a professional, a human in a parking lot) is exactly the kind of thing his API was always blind to.
  - **Voice cadence:** Turn-by-turn (spoken in the ear) + topology on the thread (blockquote). Route-physics, acronyms, "there is no such thing as a clean parking lot" energy. The cold map narrating the warm danger.

- **Donna** *(POV interrupt — the controller)*
  - **Subtext:** Command at distance is grief with a procedure attached. She cannot put her hands on the irreversible thing; she has to trust flawed men with it.   - **Voice cadence:** Dry steel, minimum volume, two-word terms. The "test it on your wrist" energy (Ch8) re-aimed to "abort if he sees the van" — domestic precision weaponized to command. NO crack here (sealed). The grief carried UNDER the command, never in it.
  - **Physical expression:** One-handed over the baby; the receiver-set-down-gently care (Ch8) re-applied to the thread; flat at the window, two fingers on the curtain — except now the window shows an empty lot and the men are gone INTO the dark she's commanding.

- **Theo** *(passenger — the cover-improv assignment)*
  - **Subtext:** He just got the role he's wanted his whole life and it's terrifying — he's the STORY if a stranger appears. The bartender's whole skill-set ("make a tense room not-tense") is, absurdly, a real infiltration skill, and he half-knows it and is half-thrilled and fully scared.
  - **Unspoken want:** To be the one who pulls it off — to matter — and to not blow it and get them caught.
  - **Voice cadence:** The flood, now *aimed* (Donna aimed it in Ch8; it stays aimed here) — he rehearses cover-stories out loud, too many of them, the comedy of a man over-preparing a lie ("okay so if somebody comes I'm — I'm the new tech, no, I'm riding along, no — Cole what's my name, do I have a name—"). - **Cole** *(driving — executing the plan)*
  - **Subtext:** Unmoved by the noise; he is sorting it into the only thing that matters (the next physical step). The chaos of the op is, to him, just inputs.
  - **Voice cadence:** SPARE. He answers the runbook with one word, ignores the rest, drives. **Flag:** the comedy of the whole beat depends on Cole NOT participating in it — his silence is the straight line the jokes break against. Do not let him deliver a quip.
