# Chapter 10 Outline

## Meta / Assumptions
- **POV + distance:** Marco — close third, present-tense interiority leaking into his CAPS/list cadence. (Justification: Ch9 spotlighted Cole on-site; Ch10's freshest threat is *comms-compromise* — the Chain itself becoming contested terrain. Marco IS the archive, the man whose ten-year text-hoard made him intel; he's the one who would NOTICE a foreign timestamp, a read-receipt that shouldn't exist, a message that arrives "wrong." Putting the listener inside his channel forces the remote man to matter, and lets the book's title object — the Chain — be felt as a violated *place* by the person who loves it most. The "no API for a man in a parking lot" lineage means the horror of a HUMAN reaching into the wire is sharpest from a systems mind.)
- **Russ-POV lock:** Honored. We never enter Russ. He is now MOVED — a returned ping, a dispatch artifact, a voice the collector administers. We feel him only as data with a tell inside it.
- **Genre lock:** Thriller-comedy every beat. Comedy = five middle-aged men trying to run a counter-surveillance op inside the group chat they now suspect is bugged, debating whether to use a code, badly inventing one, Theo over-narrating, Devang citing OPSEC he learned from a documentary. Thriller = the collector is already *inside* the safe place; courteous, frictionless, never gloats.
- **Open/close rhyme:** Opens on Marco re-reading an OLD message in the archive (the Chain as cherished home). Closes on the archive again — but now with a message in it that Marco did not save and no one sent.
- **Endgame hint:** The Russ-shaped JOB begins to surface — the collector isn't extracting money, he's using Russ's HVAC credentials/access to a specific *building* (a cold-storage / server-cooling contract). The "work" is a single job; Russ is the key, not the debt.
- **Stress voice-mutations as tells:** Marco's lists degrade under fear (numbers skip, dashes multiply); the collector's prose stays clean — its *cleanliness* is the tell.

## Setting
Marco's apartment / the wall of his archive (laptop + the Chain open on three devices, because of course he has three). Intercut: the live channel itself as a location. Late night bleeding to pre-dawn; the others scattered — Devang at his routing desk 400 mi off, Theo at the closed bar, Cole and Donna near the moved-from Sylmar site. The op runs through the wire while no two of them are in the same room.

## Opening Image
Marco scrolls UP — years up — to a dumb message from Russ in 2019 (a thermostat pun, "low and slow since '04"). The archive as warm house. Then a read-receipt blooms under a message NONE of them opened. Someone is *home* who doesn't live here.

## Chapter Hook (mid-word snatch, through the compromised channel)
The collector, having let them feel the listener, offers — courteously, through the Chain itself — a single line that is half answer, half job-description: he names the BUILDING. And as Russ's proof-of-life reply types out the word that would tell them WHY — it cuts. Not a dropped call this time: the message *delivers* truncated, the eaten word done by the wire, not the man. `> russ: it's the cold room on Osb—` and the typing indicator goes to the collector's clean hand: *"He'll finish later. We're behind schedule."* The snatch now lives in their home.

## Thematic Undercurrent
The cost of being seen. A safe place is only safe because no one is listening; the Chain survived 25 years on that lie. Comedy and grief both come from amateurs realizing their last sanctuary is staffed. And: you cannot go dark from a man who lives in your light — so you learn to *speak past* him, which is exactly what Russ taught them with the eaten word.

## Beats

1. **Open — the warm house.** Marco re-reads old Chain messages while waiting for the team to regroup post-Sylmar (the move). *Emotional target:* tenderness as armor; we love the Chain before it's violated. *character_crafter:* Marco's CAPS soften here — lowercase nostalgia, a tell that he's unguarded.

2. **The wrongness.** A read-receipt / timestamp / "delivered" that can't be right. Marco, the only one who'd catch it, catches it. He doesn't want to be right. *Emotional target:* dread entering through expertise. *Notes:* the archive-as-intel pays off — only the hoarder sees the seam.

3. **He tells the others — IN the chat — then panics that he just told the listener too.** Comedy beat: the recursive horror of warning people about the bug by using the bug. Theo immediately types three paragraphs. Devang: "STOP TYPING. (sent in the chat.)" *Emotional target:* funny-because-true terror. *character_crafter:* Marco's list fractures — "1. don't— 2. nobody— 3.—".

4. **The botched go-dark.** They try to move off-channel and are catastrophically bad at it (burner confusion, Theo calls the wrong number, Devang's "secure" method has a no-API-for-a-man problem, someone texts the op plan to a pizza place). *Emotional target:* love and incompetence. *Notes:* callback to amateurs unequipped.

5. **Donna cuts it. Minimum volume.** She forbids the panic. New doctrine: we don't run FROM the channel, we DON'T leave the house — we let him watch the wrong room. Foreman reasserts. Her crack stays sealed; one near-surface line max. *Emotional target:* steadied dread.

6. **The collector speaks in the Chain — courteous, frictionless.** First time the faceless hand types where the friends type. He doesn't gloat. He thanks them. He corrects Marco's archive on one detail (proving how deep he's read THEM). *Emotional target:* violation; the home has a tenant. *character_crafter:* collector = Russ's cadence with the love drained — clean, no caps, no fracture; the cleanliness is the menace.

7. **Counter-counter-move: feed the watched channel.** Marco's idea (the archivist's revenge — he knows what a believable lie LOOKS like because he has 25 yrs of true ones). They stage a false plan IN the Chain for the collector to read, and route the real one through Russ's own eaten-word grammar — a tell only Russ-readers parse. *Emotional target:* hope built from grief; using the dead language of a "dead" man. *Notes:* comedy in Theo's overacted fake-plan ("very normal, going to bed now, no ops here").

8. **It half-works — and reveals the JOB.** The collector takes the bait enough to relax one sentence — and in relaxing, references the WORK as routine maintenance, naming the building / the "cold room." Russ isn't a debtor being squeezed; he's a CREDENTIAL being spent on one specific access job. The team realizes the clock isn't a debt clock — it's a *schedule*. *Emotional target:* the floor drops; the shape of the endgame. *character_crafter:* Marco cross-references his archive in real time — old Russ messages about a Sylmar/Osbourne-area contract — dawning horror in list form.

9. **Russ surfaces through the wire — proof-of-life as message, not call.** He's been MOVED; now he's a typing indicator. He uses the eaten-word device ONE more time, weaponized: types the building's location to finish what the collector let slip. *Emotional target:* the man inside the equipment, reaching. POV stays OUT of his head — we read him as text only.

10. **HOOK — the snatch through the channel.** `> russ: it's the cold room on Osb—` delivers truncated; typing indicator passes to the clean hand: *"He'll finish later. We're behind schedule."* Marco scrolls — the way he opened — and finds, in the archive he curates, a message he never saved and no one sent: the collector has written *into their home.* Close-rhyme lands; the warm house now has a stranger's handwriting in it. *Emotional target:* sanctuary lost; forward-pull = WHAT is the cold room, and the snatch is now the opponent's move inside the safe place.
